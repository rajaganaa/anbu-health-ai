3. Legal Basis for Processing (DPDP Act 2023, Section 4 & 6)

We process your data only after you give free, informed, specific, and unconditional consent on first use. You can withdraw consent and delete all your data at any time (see Section 7).

4. How We Use Your Data

  • Generating AI health information responses to your questions
  • Maintaining your chat history for your convenience
  • Enforcing the daily usage limit (20 prompts/day)
  • Improving our AI models (aggregated, anonymised data only)
  • Complying with India's laws and regulations

We never: sell your data, share it with advertisers, or use it for any purpose other than above.

7. Your Rights (DPDP Act 2023, Sections 11–13)

Under India's Digital Personal Data Protection Act 2023, you have the right to:

  • Access: See what data we hold about you
  • Correction: Correct any inaccurate data
  • Erasure: Delete all your data permanently (use "Delete My Data" in app Settings)
  • Grievance: File a complaint with our Grievance Officer
  • Nominee: Designate someone to exercise your rights

To exercise rights: grievance@anbuhealth.ai

9. Security

  • All data encrypted in transit (HTTPS/TLS) and at rest (AES-256)
  • Database access protected by Row Level Security (Supabase RLS)
  • Phone authentication via Firebase OTP — no passwords stored
  • Uploaded images processed in isolated temporary storage and never persisted

10. Children's Data

Anbu Health AI is not directed at children under 18. Parents/guardians may use the app to seek health information about their children. We do not knowingly collect data from minors directly.

11. Medical AI Limitations (Telemedicine Practice Guidelines 2020)

Anbu Health AI is an AI information tool, not a registered medical practitioner. It:

  • Does NOT provide medical diagnosis
  • Does NOT write prescriptions
  • Does NOT replace consultation with a Registered Medical Practitioner
  • Should NOT be relied upon in emergencies — Call 108

We will notify you of material changes via in-app notice. Continued use after changes constitutes acceptance.
